Off-Ice Training

Off-ice training is the thing that no one really sees, but it really steps up your
game and all the work put in pays off. Off-ice training is just as effective as
actual training on-ice, and doing, for example, simple at-home workouts can
equally level up your skills as if you were training on the ice. Strengthening the
muscles with exercises involving muscles that are used in hockey, is really
important because they can make you very dominant on the ice. Keep in mind
that you don't necessarily have to be training like a bodybuilder, trying to build
muscle everywhere, whereas you are simply just trying to build more strength
in the muscles you need for hockey, but training like a bodybuilder wouldn't
necessarily be a bad thing.

Strengthening your muscles with exercises like squats and lunges, will help
build strength in your legs, which is very useful on the ice. Also, working on agility
drills is also extremely important. For example, a good and simple agility drill would
be to set up cones in a straight line, and about 2 feet apart from each other. From
the first cone, you want to quickly move side to side while going forwards as fast as
you can, and when you reach the end, you do the same thing while backwards, and
you repeat. Also, another thing that is important is doing cardio, for example going
on a run everyday, because this really helps your endurance, which is really
important when you are playing high level hockey. Other simple exercises you can
do are pushups, jumping jacks, burpees, planks, box jumps, etc.
