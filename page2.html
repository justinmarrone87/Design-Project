On-Ice Training

If you want to become better at hockey, you need to play more
often and be on the ice as much as possible. On-ice training is
very important when it comes to becoming a better hockey player.
Actually training on-ice is by far the most important action if
you want to become better. You need to be on the ice multiple
times a week to practice, and to also play games, because games
are also very important. You can do a lot of practicing, whether
it's on your shot, skating, etc. but games are still just as
important because they work on your hockey IQ and your in-game
senses, which is one of, if not the most important thing in hockey.

One of the easiest things you can do to get more ice time is to do training
sessions with a few people and with a trainer. You should do these once or
twice a week, depending on how much you’re already on the ice with your
team. These types of training sessions are usually the most beneficial,
because it is not like a team practice with your coach, and it is much more
individualized, where you can improve on just yourself. To become better,
you need to do many different drills involving your shooting abilities,
skating, stopping, your edge work, passing, use of physical contact, timing,
puck handling, deking, backwards skating, and more. With more private
sessions with trainers, you can easily work on all of these things to perfect
your game. Also, in every practice, every game, and every training session,
you have to work as hard as you can, with every single move you do,
everything has to be with 100% effort and max level intensity, or else if
you only give 75% effort, you can't get better by doing so.
